📋 Terms & Conditions

Effective Date: By registering, you accept these terms.

1. What We Collect

We collect your username, email address, and self-reported activity data (transport, food, energy, waste). We do not collect your location, device identifiers, or any sensitive personal information.

2. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used solely to compute your estimated carbon footprint, display your progress on the dashboard, and show your username on community leaderboards (by your consent via registration). We do not sell or share your data with third parties.

3. Community & Leaderboards

Your username and total carbon offset score are visible to other registered users on the community leaderboard. Your individual activity logs remain private.

4. Fair Use Policy

EcoTrace is a trust-based platform. Entering false data, abusing duplicate logs, or attempting to artificially inflate your offset score is prohibited. Duplicate walking logs are rejected server-side. Accounts found gaming the system may be removed.

5. Account & Data Deletion

You may delete your account at any time from Account Settings. Deletion permanently removes your profile, all activity logs, budgets, and challenge history. This action is irreversible.

6. Data Accuracy Disclaimer

Carbon emission estimates are based on globally recognised average emission factors and are approximations only. EcoTrace does not guarantee their accuracy for scientific or regulatory purposes.

7. Changes to These Terms

We may update these terms. Continued use of EcoTrace after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.

What is a Carbon Footprint? Your carbon footprint is the total CO2 you produce through daily activities — travel, food, energy, and waste. A negative value (e.g. −0.1 kg) means you've offset more carbon than you emitted — this is great! It happens when you complete eco-challenges or log walking steps. The goal is to reduce gross emissions and increase offsets, keeping your net footprint as low as possible.
